What's in the model

  • ▸1,529 DU 2025-26 Round 1 & Round 3 cutoffs across all 67 colleges
  • ▸NTA 2025 + 2026 subject-wise max-score data
  • ▸Category-pool growth (UR/OBC/SC/ST/EWS/PwBD)
  • ▸Top-end density (100%-ile holders ↑20% YoY)
  • ▸Structural changes (e.g. BFIA +1 domain in 2026)
  • ▸Seat scarcity premium for tiny UR/EWS pools

The math, in plain English

  1. 12025 cutoff = score of last-admitted student → corresponds to a fixed percentile of the pool
  2. 2Project that same percentile onto the 2026 composite scale (with formula & subject inflation)
  3. 3Adjust upward for pool growth × category competition × top-end density
  4. 4Probability = sigmoid((you − cutoff) / σ), where σ is tier-aware
  5. 5Confidence ↓ when structure changed or 2026 data sparse
